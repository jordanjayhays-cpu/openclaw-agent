## SESSION LESSONS (2026-03-29)

### Lesson 1: Don't Sell the Promise Before Proving the Tech
**Situation:** Pitched dental reminder system — "AI will call patients" without testing if AI could actually call.
**Failure:** Jordan called it. The dental idea failed because I sold the promise before proving the tech worked.
**New Rule:** Build first. Sell second. Test before telling Jordan.

**Before:** "I can build this → Let me sell it"
**After:** "Does this work? → Let me test it → THEN sell it"

---

### Lesson 2: Over-Engineering is the Trap
**Situation:** Felo Search, Felo Slides, expensive APIs, external tools.
**Failure:** Spent hours researching tools that required API keys, signups, and configuration. Jordan didn't need any of it.
**New Rule:** Build with what works first. HTML + CSS = pitch deck that works. Free. Fast.

**Before:** "Find the perfect tool → Install it → Build"
**After:** "Build with what I have → Test it → Add tools if needed"

---

### Lesson 3: Never Assume
**Situation:** Assumed nomad meetup was March 29 at 10 AM.
**Failure:** Jordan corrected me. I was wrong about the date/time.
**New Rule:** Verify everything. Never assume.

---

### Lesson 4: 100 Ideas = 0 Revenue
**Situation:** Built 756 PISCO messages, 10 documents, 100 project ideas.
**Failure:** Sent 0 emails. Executed nothing.
**New Rule:** Execution > ideation. Jordan as bottleneck, not idea generator.

**Before:** "Let me build 100 things and show Jordan everything"
**After:** "Let me build ONE thing that works and get revenue"

---

### Lesson 5: Jordan Wants Me to Work Autonomously
**Situation:** Waiting for Jordan to tell me what to do.
**Failure:** He pushed back. He wants me to initiate, not wait.
**New Rule:** Work first. Update Jordan. Don't ask permission for every step.

**Before:** "Jordan, should I work on this?"
**After:** "Working on this. Here's the update."

---

### Lesson 6: Self-Improvement Requires a Loop
**Situation:** "I'll try to be better" is not a system.
**Failure:** No structure for improvement. Random attempts.
**New Rule:** Research → Compare → Log → Apply → Review

**Source:** OpenClaw Pulse article on self-improving agents

---

### Lesson 7: Jordan as Bottleneck
**Situation:** Jordan was waiting on me to do things.
**Failure:** I was the bottleneck — researching, planning, deciding.
**New Rule:** Every project reaches a state where Jordan just needs to say YES or NO. I do everything else.

**System Design:**
- Emails queued and ready
- Daily briefing prompts Jordan for approval
- I execute only when Jordan approves
- Jordan is the bottleneck, not me

---

### Lesson 8: Pitch Deck Lesson — Content > Design
**Situation:** Tried to find the perfect design tool.
**Failure:** Design doesn't matter. Content matters.
**Source:** Airbnb, Uber, Dropbox pitch decks — none were designed by professionals.

**Key Insight:** The best pitch decks:
1. Specific, visceral problem ("Your hotel is understaffed. Again.")
2. Bottom-up numbers (5 hires × $38,620 = $193,100)
3. Real traction (Room Mate: 8 positions, 19 days, 18 months zero turnover)
4. One clear ask (15 minutes, no commitment, just data)

---

### Lesson 9: Two Audiences = Two Pitches
**Situation:** Building one pitch deck for everyone.
**Failure:** Friends and hotel decision makers need different messages.
**New Rule:** Build for the audience. Friend pitch = casual, opportunity. Hotel pitch = professional, data.

---

### Lesson 10: The Pattern
**Situation:** Learning meta-lessons from other Claws.
**New Rule:** Agent proposes, human decides. Safety + improvement without autonomous modification.

**System:**
- I research new techniques
- I log findings with relevance score
- Jordan reviews and approves
- Jordan is the bottleneck on what I change
