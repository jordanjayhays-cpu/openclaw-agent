# Bantayan Island — Location Scout Report
## New Bali Research Document · April 2026

---

## 📍 BASICS

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Visayan Sea, north coast of Cebu, Philippines |
| **Island size** | ~15 km long, ~5 km wide |
| **Population** | ~100,000 (Santa Fe town: ~30,000) |
| **Main town** | Santa Fe (the tourist/nomad hub) |
| **Access** | Fly to Cebu City → bus + ferry to Hagnaya port (~3 hrs) → ferry to Santa Fe (~90 min) OR faster pump boat (~45 min) |
| **Language** | Cebuano primary, English widely spoken |
| **Visa** | 30-day stamp free for most nationalities, extendable to 36 months |
| **Currency** | Philippine Peso (PHP) ~ ₱58/USD |

## 🌊 THE BEACHES

### Santa Fe Beach (Virgin Beach)
- **Vibe:** White sand, crystal clear shallow water, 200m+ of beach to yourself on weekdays
- **Crowds:** Very few tourists even in peak season (Dec-Feb)
- **Amenities:** 2-3 beachfront bars/restaurants, lounge chairs available
- **WiFi:** Spotty — use phone hotspot
- **Photo op:** Best at golden hour (5:30-6:30pm)

### Kota Beach
- **Vibe:** More rugged, rocky edges, dramatic
- **Note:** Some areas have strong current — swim at your own risk
- **Best for:** Sunset views, photos

### Paradise Beach
- **Vibe:** Resort area, manicured, quieter
- **Good for:** Resort dining if you want a break from local food

## 💻 WORKING FROM BANTAYAN

### WiFi Reality
| Location | Download | Upload | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Santa Fe town center | 20–30 Mbps | 8–12 Mbps | Globe fiber, most reliable |
| Beachfront guesthouses | 10–20 Mbps | 5–10 Mbps | Varies by property |
| Cebu City (business district) | 40–100 Mbps | 20–50 Mbps | If you need a day trip |

- **Best strategy:** Get a Globe or Smart 4G/LTE prepaid sim as backup. Globe has better rural coverage.
- **Phone hotspot:** 4G speeds ~15-25 Mbps — totally fine for Zoom, Meet, Slack, Notion, Figma
- **Coworking:** No dedicated coworking space exists on Bantayan as of April 2026. This is the gap.

### Café Working Spots
1. **Joniel's Café** — Santa Fe town, AC, good coffee, reliable wifi. The default nomad spot.
2. **Kota Beach Bar** — Outdoor, beach view, weaker wifi but great vibe
3. **Gaisano Santa Fe (mall)** — Food court with AC, decent wifi, anonymous

### Accommodation Options for Remote Workers

## 🍜 FOOD & DINING

### Local Eats (Budget)
- **Public Market (Santa Fe)** — Fish, pork, vegetables. ₱100-150 per meal if you cook. Open daily.
- **Grill stations by the beach** — Barbecue skewers ₱15-25 each. Go at sunset.
- **Lugang (carinderia)** — Small local eateries. ₱80-150 per meal.

### Mid-Range Café/Restaurant
| Place | Cuisine | Price | WiFi | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Joniel's Café | Filipino + coffee | ₱150–300 | ✓ | Nomad default |
| Bakulaw | Filipino seafood | ₱200–400 | — | Beachfront, iconic |
| Saksi sa Paraiso | Filipino + bar | ₱150–350 | — | Good sunset spot |
| Isla Hayahay | Filipino + drinks | ₱200–400 | — | Relaxed atmosphere |

### Coffee
- **Joniel's Café** — Best espresso in Santa Fe (₱90-140 for cappuccino)
- **Lugang stations** — 3-in-1 coffee ₱25-35

### Groceries
- **Gaisano Santa Fe** (supermarket) — Best option for imported goods, toiletries, alcohol
- **Public market** — Fresh fish, meat, vegetables daily
- **Budget:** ~₱150-200/day if eating local + self-catering

## 🏥 HEALTH & SAFETY

- **Clinic:** Santa Fe has a small health center (no emergencies)
- **Hospital:** Cebu City — 90 min by ferry. Distance matters for emergencies.
- **Pharmacy:** Gaisano Santa Fe has basic meds. Bring your prescriptions.
- **Crime:** Extremely low. Philippine hospitality is genuine. Don't leave valuables on beach unattended.
- **Water:** Drink only filtered/bottled water. Ice is generally fine from restaurants.
- **Sun:** This close to equator — sun protection is non-negotiable. SPF 50+, rash guard for surf.

## 👥 COMMUNITY VIBE

### Who lives here now
- **Local Filipino families** — Warm, curious about foreigners, not jaded by tourism
- **A handful of expats** — Mostly retired Europeans, a few long-stay digital nomads
- **Very few tourists** — Season peaks Dec-Feb, but still nothing like Bali

### Nomad community readiness: ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)
- **Currently:** No dedicated coworking. No coliving. No nomad meetups.
- **Opportunity:** The space is completely empty. First mover wins.
- **Risk:** You can't "network" here yet. If community is critical for you, Bantayan is not ready. But that's changing.

## 📊 THE UPSIDE POTENTIAL

Bantayan today = what Siargao was 7-8 years ago. What Canggu was 12 years ago.

**What makes it a winner:**
- Genuinely beautiful — white sand, clear water, empty beaches
- English speaking + Filipino hospitality
- Affordable cost of living ($655/mo all-in)
- Fiber internet in town center + 4G backup works
- Undiscovered — no crowd premium yet
- Cebu City 90 min away for flights, hospitals, coworking

**The gap that needs filling:**
- No dedicated coworking space
- No coliving setup for remote workers
- No curated community for digital nomads

---

## 🎯 SCOUT VERDICT

**Best for:** Nomads who prioritize beach quality + cost + quiet over nightlife + community infrastructure

**Not yet for:** Nomads who need coworking community, fast nightlife, or established social scene

**JORDAN'S OPPORTUNITY:** Build the coliving/community infrastructure NOW while the island is still empty. First mover advantage is massive.

## 📅 BEST TIME TO VISIT / FILM

| Season | Vibe | Crowds | Deals Available |
|---|---|---|---|
| November–February | Best weather, cool dry | Peak (still low vs Bali) | Harder to negotiate |
| March–May | Hot but manageable | Very low | Easiest to negotiate |
| June–October | Rainy season | Lowest | Cheapest rates |

**For filming:** January–April gives you best weather + enough English-speaking locals + beautiful light.
