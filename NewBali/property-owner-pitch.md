## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

**"I don't want strangers in my property."**
> That's exactly why we pre-vet everyone. New Bali guests are remote workers, not backpackers. We filter for people who want to stay 30+ days and are serious about their work. We've had zero incident reports with any of our guests.

**"I can just list on Airbnb."**
> You can. But Airbnb guests are transactional — they book for a weekend, leave a review, and you start over. New Bali guests stay 1–3 months, come back, and refer their colleagues. The lifetime value of a nomad tenant is completely different from a tourist booking.

**"I need to think about it."**
> Of course — take the time you need. The one thing I'd mention: we're filming in [Month]. If you want to be in the documentary, we need to lock in the property by [Date]. After that, the video is already shot.

**"What's the commission rate again?"**
> We're proposing [X]% on New Bali platform bookings. For the first 6 months as a founding partner, we'd reduce that to [X]% in exchange for the promotional feature and exclusive early access to our waitlist.

## FINAL REMINDER

Always:
- Keep emails short (under 200 words each)
- Lead with what THEY get, not what YOU want
- Attach real social proof (your YouTube subscriber count, any press mentions)
- Offer a call — always
- Never beg, never hard sell
