# NEW BALI — YouTube Video Script
**Format:** Vlog / Discovery Documentary  
**Target Length:** 12–18 minutes  
**Hook:** "I found the place Bali used to be — and it's $600/month"

---

**[Fast cuts: beach at golden hour, laptop on balcony, local market, fast wifi speedtest]**

> "This is Bantayan Island. And by the end of this video, you're going to want to book a flight."

**[Thumbnail: aerial beach shot + text overlay "BALI IS OVER. THIS IS THE REPLACEMENT."]**

## INTRO (0:30 – 1:30)
**[On camera, sitting at a beachfront café]**

> "My name's Jordan. I've been a digital nomad for [X years]. I run a couple of businesses online, and I've been to Bali four times. The first time was magical. The last time — same place, same guesthouse — cost me three times as much and felt like a theme park."
>
> "Six months ago I started looking for what's next. Thailand? Too saturated. Portugal? Already expensive. Then a friend who lives in Cebu said: 'You need to check out Bantayan.'"
>
> "So I did. And this video is everything I found."

**[B-roll: ferry ride to Bantayan, arriving at the island, first impressions]**

---

## THE LOCATION (1:30 – 5:00)
### What is Bantayan Island?
**[On camera + drone shots]**

> "Bantayan is a small island in the Visayan Sea, off the north coast of Cebu in the Philippines. Population: about 100,000. Tourist numbers: low. English: widely spoken. Wifi: surprisingly decent."

### What it looks like
**[B-roll: white sand beaches, clear water, local town, fishing boats]**

> "The beaches here are white sand, crystal clear water, and genuinely empty — we're talking 200 meters of beach to yourself on a Tuesday morning. It looks like Boracay did 20 years ago before it got ruined."
>
> "The local town — Santa Fe — has everything you need. Markets, good local food, motorbike rentals, a couple of decent cafes with AC."

### The Downsides (be honest)
> "Let me be real: this isn't Canggu. There's no coworking space with a pool. Nightlife is basically zero. The nearest hospital is in Cebu City, 90 minutes by ferry. And if you need fast fashion or your specific brand of oat milk, forget it."
>
> "But if you want to actually work, live well, and save money — this place is a gift."

---

## COST OF LIVING (5:00 – 7:30)
**[On camera, screen-share of budget spreadsheet]**

### Jordan's actual monthly costs:
| Category | Amount |
|---|---|
| Accommodation (1BR, furnished) | $350 |
| Fast WiFi (fiber, 30Mbps) | $25 |
| Food (local restaurants + groceries) | $200 |
| Motorbike rental | $50 |
| Ferries + transport | $30 |
| **Total** | **~$655/month** |

**[On camera]**

> "That's everything. Not a budget breakdown pretending rice and beans is a lifestyle. That's eating well, drinking coffee every day, working from cafes, and going out."
>
> "For context: a comparable setup in Canggu, Bali right now is running people $1,800–2,200 a month. In Siargao — which I also visited — more like $1,200–1,500."
>
> "Bantayan is the cheapest genuine digital nomad destination I've found in Southeast Asia that actually has good internet."

---

## WORKING FROM BANTYAN (7:30 – 10:00)
**[Screen record: Zoom call, speed tests, working from beachfront café]**

### WiFi Reality
> "I ran speed tests at six different locations. Average: 25 Mbps down, 10 Mbps up. Not fiber — but totally fine for Zoom, Google Meet, Slack, and most cloud tools."
>
> "For video calls: I used my phone as a hotspot 4G backup. It was flawless."

### Coworking Options
> "Right now there's no dedicated coworking space on Bantayan. But there are two cafes with good AC, reliable wifi, and decent coffee — and you can rent private rooms in some guesthouses by the day."

### Why This is a Business Opportunity
> "Here's the thing: someone could put a decent coworking space here for $50,000–$80,000. 15–20 desks, fiber internet, a kitchen, a rooftop. You'd fill it in six months just from YouTube and Nomad List traffic."
>
> "This is exactly what we're building with New Bali. But more on that in a second."

---

## THE COMMUNITY ANGLE (10:00 – 12:00)
**[On camera, walking through town]**

> "The thing Bali had — and lost — was the feeling that everyone was in the same boat. Travelers, freelancers, small business owners, all figuring it out together."
>
> "Bantayan has that energy right now. The local community is warm and curious. The few other remote workers I've met here are genuinely interesting people who came for the right reasons."
>
> "That's what we're trying to preserve. Not another overpriced backpacker hub — an actual community of people who want to live well and work from somewhere beautiful."
