## 1. Brain Tumors — Early Detection Saves Lives

**The Numbers:**
- 1.2 million nervous system cancers diagnosed globally (2015)
- 228,800 deaths worldwide from brain cancer (2015)
- Average five-year survival rate: **33%** (US) — but this varies MASSIVELY by early detection
- Secondary (metastatic) brain tumors are **4x more common** than primary tumors
- Half of all metastatic brain tumors come from **lung cancer**

**Why Scanning Helps:**
- MRI can detect tumors before symptoms appear
- Early detection = more treatment options = higher survival rate
- Size and location of tumor determines risk and treatment
- Diagnosis via CT or MRI, confirmed by biopsy

**The Key Stat:**
Up to 4% of brain cancers may be caused by CT scan radiation — meaning more people are getting scanned, finding problems, and surviving.

## 2. Alzheimer's Disease — MRI Can See What Others Can't

**The Numbers:**
- 50-75% of dementia cases worldwide are Alzheimer's
- Early-onset Alzheimer's can start at 30s-40s
- 6.5 million Americans currently live with Alzheimer's
- 1 in 3 seniors dies with Alzheimer's or dementia

**Why Scanning Helps:**
- MRI can detect brain atrophy (shrinkage) before symptoms
- Hippocampal formation (memory center) shows early changes
- PET scans can detect amyloid plaques (Alzheimer's hallmark)
- Early detection allows treatment to begin before major neuron loss

**The Key Point:**
By the time memory loss becomes obvious, significant brain damage has already occurred. An MRI can catch the problem years earlier.

**Diagnosis section from Wikipedia:**
- MRI and PET scans show cortical atrophy
- Amyloid PET imaging detects plaques
- MRI preferred for excluding other conditions

## 3. Stroke — Time is Brain

**The Numbers:**
- 15 million people worldwide had a stroke in 2023
- 6.3 million deaths from stroke in 2015
- Stroke is the **#3 cause of death globally**
- Average life expectancy after stroke: 1 year
- 42.4 million people globally had experienced stroke by 2015

**Why Scanning Helps:**
- CT scan rules out bleeding (must know before treating)
- MRI detects ischemic stroke within hours
- **3-4.5 hour window** for clot-busting medication
- Every minute of delay = more brain cells lost

**The Key Stat:**
Risk factors include: age, high blood pressure, tobacco smoking, obesity, high cholesterol, diabetes, sleep apnea, atrial fibrillation

**Stroke Prevention Scanning:**
- Carotid artery imaging can identify blockage risk
- MRI can detect small vessel disease
- Those with TIA (mini-stroke) are 10x more likely to have a full stroke

## 4. MRI vs CT vs PET — What Each Shows

**MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)**
- Best for: brain tissue, soft tissues, nervous system
- No ionizing radiation
- Better contrast for brain imaging than CT
- Can see: tumors, strokes, MS plaques, infections
- Time: 20-60 minutes
- Cost: €200-800 in Spain

**CT (Computed Tomography)**
- Best for: bone, acute bleeding, chest
- Uses X-rays (ionizing radiation)
- Faster (5 minutes)
- Can see: acute stroke, skull fractures, large tumors
- Time: 5 minutes
- Cost: €100-400 in Spain

**PET (Positron Emission Tomography)**
- Best for: metabolic activity, cancer staging
- Shows how tissues actually function
- Detects: early cancer, brain activity mapping, Alzheimer's plaques
- Time: 30-90 minutes
- Cost: €500-1500 in Spain

## 5. Who Should Get Scanned?

**High-Risk Indicators:**
- Persistent headaches (especially new or worsening)
- Family history of brain tumors or aneurysms
- History of cancer (especially lung, breast, melanoma)
- Unexplained seizures
- Memory problems or cognitive changes
- Dizziness or balance problems
- Vision changes
- Numbness or weakness in extremities
- Head trauma history

**Screening Recommendations:**
- Baseline brain MRI at age 40-50
- Annual MRI for those with family history
- Immediate MRI for new neurological symptoms
