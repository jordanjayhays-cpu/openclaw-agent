# NeuroMatch Competitor Analysis

**Research Date:** April 21, 2026
**Purpose:** Identify the competitive landscape for a brain scan/MRI booking marketplace ("NeuroMatch")

---

## Executive Summary

The brain scan/MRI booking marketplace is nascent but rapidly evolving. Most existing players either:
1. Focus on **telemedicine radiology interpretation** (B2B, hospital-facing) — not patient-facing booking
2. Offer **self-pay preventive full-body scans** (USA only, no Spain/Europe presence)
3. Are **hospital/practice internal systems** — not consumer marketplaces
4. Focus on **imaging centers** — booking infrastructure, not radiology interpretation

**Key Gap:** There is NO patient-facing brain scan marketplace connecting patients directly with both imaging centers AND radiology interpretation services in Spain/Europe.

## US-Based Companies

### 1. **Ezra** 🟢 (Strong Competitor in Preventive Scans)
- **What they do:** Full-body MRI screening for cancer and abnormalities. Patient books MRI at one of 170+ partner imaging centers. Radiologists interpret and deliver reports.
- **Model:** Patient pays directly (self-pay). Subscription model for ongoing monitoring.
- **Pricing:** ~$1,350-$1,500 for full body scan, additional $250-500 for MRI contrast agents
- **Coverage:** 13 organs including brain and prostate. Uses both MRI and CT depending on organ.
- **Radiology:** Board-certified radiologists provide interpretation within 10 days
- **Presence:** USA only (170+ imaging centers nationwide)
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:** 
  - ✓ Similar direct-to-patient model
  - ✓ Focus on preventive/wellness scanning (not clinical indication)
  - ✗ USA only — no European expansion
  - ✗ Full-body focus, not brain-specific
  - ✗ No neurological subspecialty emphasis
  - **Opportunity for NeuroMatch:** European focus, neurological subspecialty (neuroradiologist reading brain MRI specifically)

### 2. **vRad (Virtual Radiologic)** 🔵 (B2B Teleradiology, Not Direct Patient)
- **What they do:** Provides 24/7 radiology interpretation services to hospitals and imaging centers. NOT patient-facing.
- **Model:** B2B — hospitals and imaging centers contract vRad for overnight/weekend coverage and subspecialty reads
- **Scale:** 115M+ patients served, 25 patents, 25+ AI models, 140+ communities for breast imaging
- **Radiology:** US-based, fellowship-trained radiologists
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:**
  - ✗ B2B only — no direct patient booking
  - ✗ Interpretation services for providers, not patients
  - **Key insight:** vRad represents the teleradiology interpretation infrastructure layer — NeuroMatch could partner with or replicate this model

### 3. **Nighthawk** 🔵 (Historical Teleradiology Pioneer)
- **What they do:** First company to station US-licensed radiologists overseas (Australia, Switzerland) to provide night-call coverage for US hospitals.
- **Model:** B2B — hospital contracts for overnight radiology coverage
- **Status:** Acquired and integrated into larger radiology groups; less relevant as direct competitor
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:** Historical model only — not a marketplace

### 4. **Sesame** 🟡 (Broad Telehealth, Some Imaging)
- **What they do:** Direct-to-consumer telehealth platform offering urgent care, mental health, weight loss, prescriptions. Recently added imaging.
- **Model:** Patient pays per visit (self-pay, no insurance)
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:**
  - ✓ Self-pay marketplace model
  - ✗ Not imaging-focused, more general telehealth
  - ✗ No brain scan specific offering

### 5. **RAD365** 🔵 (Teleradiology Workflow)
- **What they do:** End-to-end radiology workflow, AI workflow, PACS solutions
- **Model:** B2B software/AI workflow for radiology practices
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:** Infrastructure layer, not a patient marketplace

## Europe/Spain-Based Companies

### 6. **No European Equivalent Found** ⚠️

**Critical Finding:** There is NO European company operating a direct-to-patient brain scan marketplace with radiology interpretation. This is a significant market gap.

**What exists in Europe:**
- National health systems (SNS in Spain, NHS in UK, public systems elsewhere) — long waits, no direct booking
- Private radiology chains (e.g., Capio, Affidea in Europe) — bookable but no consumer-facing marketplace with radiology interpretation bundled
- Hospital-facing teleradiology services (same as US — B2B)

**Spain-Specific:**
- Private health insurance is common (Sanitas, Adeslas, Mapfre, etc.)
- Imaging often requires doctor referral first
- Self-pay imaging market underdeveloped
- No known direct-to-consumer brain scan booking platform

### 7. **Affidea** 🔵 (European Imaging Chain)
- **What they do:** Widespread European imaging center network (Ireland, UK, Netherlands, Belgium, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Poland, Romania, Hungary)
- **Model:** B2B with some direct patient; insurance-based primarily
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:**
  - ✓ European presence (including Spain)
  - ✗ Infrastructure/network, not a consumer marketplace
  - ✗ No bundled radiology interpretation with booking

### 8. **Capio** 🔵 (European Imaging Chain)
- **What they do:** Private healthcare provider with imaging centers across Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Germany, France
- **Model:** Mix of public funding and insurance
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:** Same as Affidea — operational centers, not a marketplace

## Marketplace/Tech-Enabled Models

### 9. **Zocdoc** 🟡 (General Medical Booking — Adjacent)
- **What they do:** Online appointment booking for all medical specialties in USA
- **Model:** Patient books, sometimes pays through platform; doctors pay for presence
- **Comparison to NeuroMatch:**
  - ✓ Marketplace model
  - ✗ General medical, not imaging-specific
  - ✗ Not radiology interpretation
  - ✗ USA only
  - **Lesson:** Zocdoc proved patient demand for online booking but imaging requires different trust/credibility signals

### 10. **Other MRI Booking Platforms (USA)**
Companies that do online MRI/CT booking:
- Many regional players (google "MRI near me booking" returns regional imaging centers with online booking)

## Gaps in the Market (NeuroMatch Opportunity)

### 1. **No European Patient-Facing Brain Scan Marketplace**
- Europe has no Ezra equivalent
- National health systems have long waits (weeks to months for non-urgent MRI)
- Private imaging exists but no consumer booking with bundled interpretation

### 2. **No Neurological Subspecialty Focus**
- Ezra does full-body cancer screening
- Most teleradiology is general radiologists doing overnight coverage
- Neuroradiologists (sub-specialists) are underutilized in consumer-facing platforms
- NeuroMatch can differentiate on **brain-specific, neuroradiologist-read scans**

### 3. **No Self-Pay Brain MRI in Europe**
- In the USA, self-pay MRI ranges $400-$3,000 depending on type
- In Spain, private MRI costs €200-€600 but requires doctor referral and no bundled interpretation
- Market opportunity: direct patient booking without referral + bundled neuroradiologist interpretation

### 4. **No Marketplace Model for Brain Imaging**
- Existing models are either:
  - Imaging center (point solution)
  - Teleradiology for hospitals (B2B)
  - Full-body wellness (different market)
- **Marketplace gap:** Aggregates multiple imaging centers + interpretation + follow-up

### 5. **Telemedicine Radiology Interpretation**
- Most teleradiology is hospital-to-hospital (night coverage)
- Patient directly accessing teleradiology interpretation is rare
- This is the key value-add: patient books scan → platform arranges → neuroradiologist reads → patient gets report

## Recommendations for NeuroMatch

### Immediate Competitive Intelligence Gaps to Fill:
1. **Spanish private imaging center partnerships** — need to understand pricing, availability, referral requirements
2. **European teleradiology staffing** — which firms provide neuroradiology coverage?
3. **Regulatory requirements** — Spain specifically: can patients self-refer for MRI? What are reporting requirements?

### Potential Partners/Acquirers:
- European teleradiology groups (many exist, often small/mid-size)
- Private imaging chains (Affidea Spain, Capio, regional players)
- Health insurance companies (interested in preventive health options)

### Competitive Moat:
1. **Neuroradiology network** — hard to replicate, requires medical credibility
2. **Spanish/European market focus** — first mover advantage
3. **Simple patient journey** — no referral needed, one-click booking, clear pricing
4. **Interpretation quality** — fellowship-trained neuroradiologists (not general radiologists)

### Pricing Benchmarks (Self-Pay MRI, USA):
- Brain MRI without contrast: $400-$800
- Brain MRI with contrast: $600-$1,200
- Ezra full body: $1,350-$1,500
- Spain private MRI: €200-€600 (but no direct booking + bundled interpretation)

### European Regulatory Considerations:
- **Spain:** Patients generally need doctor referral for MRI (but this varies by region/type)
- **EU Medical Device Regulations** may apply to software interpretation platforms
- **GDPR** requirements for medical data
- **Teleradiology regulations** vary by country — most require radiologist licensing in the country of patient
