SLEEP — HUBERMAN LAB TOOLKIT
====================================================================

Andrew Huberman (Stanford neuroscientist) compiled the most
evidence-based sleep protocol available.

**MORNING — Within 30 minutes of waking:**
- Get 10-30 minutes of sunlight (even cloudy days work)
- This sets your circadian rhythm
- Don't wear sunglasses (block the signal)
- Don't look directly at sun (just be outside/in light)

**AVOID first 90 minutes of waking:**
- Caffeine (wait 90+ min after waking)
- Your cortisol is high naturally in the morning
- Adding caffeine disrupts cortisol pattern

**EVENING — Before bed:**
- Dim lights, especially blue light
- Cool room: 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- Dark room: blackout curtains or eye mask
- No screens or use blue light filter

**SUPPLEMENTS (optional):**
- Magnesium threonate (best for sleep)
- Apigenin (chamomile extract)
- Theanine (relaxation)
- "Sleep cocktail" before bed: magnesium + apigenin + theanine

**CONSISTENCY:**
- Same bedtime and wake time daily (even weekends)
- Your body craves predictability

Source: hubermanlab.com/newsletter/toolkit-for-sleep

**ACTION:** Get morning sunlight. Keep bedroom cool and dark.
Stick to a sleep schedule.

EXERCISE — MINIMUM EFFECTIVE DOSE
====================================================================

You don't need to live in the gym.

**MINIMUM EFFECTIVE DOSE research shows:**
- 10-15 minutes of exercise per day
- OR 3-5 times per week
- Reduces all-cause mortality by ~14%

**"WEEKEND WARRIOR" works:**
- Even exercising 1-2 days per week shows benefits
- Better than nothing
- As long as you actually do it

**Resistance training (strength):**
- Minimum: 4 sets of 6-15 reps per muscle group per week
- OR 2-3 sets of 6-12 reps per exercise per week
- Builds muscle, maintains metabolism

**Aerobic/cardio:**
- 150 min moderate OR 75 min vigorous per week
- That's 30 min/day x 5 days (brisk walking counts)
- Even 10 min bursts help

**KEY INSIGHT:**
"Any exercise is better than no exercise."
Start small. Consistency beats intensity.

Source: ACSM Health & Fitness Journal, Nature Medicine 2024

**ACTION:** 10-15 min/day minimum. Walk more. Strength train 2x/week.

FLOW STATE — MIHALY CSIKSZENTMIHALYI
====================================================================

Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi studied "flow" — the state of complete
absorption where time disappears and performance peaks.

**THE 8 COMPONENTS OF FLOW:**
1. Clear goals
2. Immediate feedback
3. Challenge-skill balance (not too easy, not too hard)
4. Merging of action and awareness
5. Intense concentration
6. Sense of control
7. Loss of self-consciousness
8. Transformation of time

**HOW TO ENTER FLOW:**
1. Match challenge level to your skill
   - Too easy = boredom
   - Too hard = anxiety
   - Just right = flow

2. Clear goals before starting
   - Know exactly what you're doing and why

3. Eliminate distractions
   - Phone off, notifications blocked
   - Deep work environment

4. Get immediate feedback
   - Know how you're doing as you go

5. Deep focus
   - Practice staying in the moment

**FLOW TRIGGERS:**
- High concentration
- Challenge-skill balance
- Clear goals
- Immediate feedback
- Sense of control
- Paradoxical control (letting go to gain control)

Source: positivepsychology.com, Csikszentmihalyi research

**ACTION:** Match challenge to skill. Eliminate distractions. Practice deep work.

SUNLIGHT — COLD/HEAT EXPOSURE
====================================================================

**MORNING SUNLIGHT (already covered in sleep):**
- Sets circadian rhythm
- Boosts serotonin
- Regulates melatonin
- 10-30 min within 30 min of waking

**COLD EXPOSURE:**
- Cold showers, ice baths, cold plunges
- Benefits: reduced inflammation, increased focus, better mood
- Start: 30 seconds cold at end of shower
- Build up gradually

**HEAT EXPOSURE:**
- Sauna, hot bath, hot yoga
- Benefits: cardiovascular health, longevity, relaxation
- 15-20 min sessions
- 2-3 times per week

**BOTTOM LINE:**
Morning sunlight is non-negotiable.
Cold and heat exposure are accelerants.

Source: Huberman Lab, NIH research

**ACTION:** Morning sunlight. Occasional cold. Occasional heat.

NUTRITION — EATING WINDOW
====================================================================

**TIME-RESTRICTED EATING:**
- Confine eating to 10-12 hour window
- Example: 8am-8pm or 9am-7pm
- More than 8 hours may increase cardiovascular risk
- Less than 8 hours may be too restrictive

**WHAT WORKS:**
- 10-12 hour eating window = benefits
- Consistent meal timing
- Don't eat 2-3 hours before bed

**WHAT DOESN'T WORK (well):**
- Extreme fasting (>16 hours daily)
- 8-hour eating window linked to higher CV risk in one study
- Obsessive tracking

**BOTTOM LINE:**
- Eat in a 10-12 hour window
- Stop eating 2-3 hours before bed
- Don't stress about perfection

Source: Lancet, American Heart Association 2024, Nature Medicine

**ACTION:** 10-12 hour eating window. No food 2-3 hrs before bed.

MINDSET — GROWTH VS FIXED
====================================================================

Carol Dweck (Stanford psychologist) research:
- Fixed mindset: "I'm born this way, can't change"
- Growth mindset: "I can improve through effort"

**GROWTH MINDSET BENEFITS:**

- Better learning
- Higher achievement

**HOW TO CULTIVATE:**
- See challenges as growth opportunities
- Learn from failures (don't label yourself)
- Say "yet" — "I can't do this YET"
- Effort is path to mastery

**FOR JORDAN:**

- That's growth mindset in action

Source: Carol Dweck, Mindset research

**ACTION:** Frame setbacks as feedback. Say "yet." Keep building.
