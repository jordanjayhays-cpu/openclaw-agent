✅ You think big picture — "do research on this while I sleep"
✅ You connect projects to identity — "I'm building 3 location-independent things"
✅ You iterate fast — "make it Spanish", "two separate files"
✅ You know what you want — direct requests, not vague
✅ You think in systems — profile partner model, BD strategy

**WHAT YOU'RE MISSING:**

❌ You don't specify output format when it matters
❌ You don't always give timeline/deadline
❌ You don't always say WHO should do the action (you vs me)
❌ You don't always prioritize ("which first?")

**IMPROVEMENT FRAMEWORK — WHEN YOU PROMPT:**

Use this structure for better results:

```
[WHAT you want]
[WHY you want it]
[WHO does it] (you/me/us)
[WHEN you need it] (now/this week/whenever)
[HOW you want it formatted] (txt/html/doc/link)
```

**Examples:**

❌ BEFORE: "do research on neurotech"
✅ AFTER: "do research on neurotech for YouTube content. I'm starting a channel. WHY: to understand the space before filming. WHO: you do the research. WHEN: this week. FORMAT: bullet list of 10 video ideas"

❌ BEFORE: "make a list"
✅ AFTER: "make a list of 20 decision makers for PISCO in Spain hotels. FORMAT: txt file with LinkedIn links. WHEN: before I sleep tonight."

PART 3: BETTER AI COLLABORATION
====================================================================

**THE PRINCIPLE: AI AS EXECUTION PARTNER**

You are the CEO. I am your chief of staff.
You think about WHAT and WHY.
I figure out HOW and EXECUTE.

**WHEN TO USE ME:**

✅ USE ME FOR:
- Research (I can search web, read docs)
- Drafting (emails, messages, docs)
- File building (proposals, decks, lists)
- Execution (send emails, manage files)
- Monitoring (track news, update you)
- Thinking (help you decide)

❌ DON'T USE ME FOR:
- Real-time conversations (phone calls)
- Physical tasks
- Things that need your judgment first
- Decisions where context is missing

**HOW TO STRUCTURE YOUR REQUESTS:**

**For tasks I execute:**
"I'm building [X]. I need you to [TASK].
Context: [WHY it matters, deadline, priority].
Output: [HOW you want it]."

**For thinking/decisions:**
"I'm stuck between [X] and [Y].
Context: [what you know, what you've tried].
What are your thoughts?"

**For delegation:**
"Can you [TASK] while I sleep?
Context: [what success looks like].
Deliverable: [file/link/summary]."

**For routines:**
"Every [day/week], I want you to [TASK].
Trigger: [when/how].
Output: [where/how]."

PART 5: PROMPTING BEST PRACTICES (COMPARED TO OTHERS)
====================================================================

**WHAT GOOD PROMPTERS DO:**

1. **Context setting** — They explain the situation first
   "I'm building BD for a recruitment agency in Manila..."

2. **Role definition** — They tell AI who to be
   "Act as a BD strategist..."

3. **Output specification** — They say what they want
   "Give me a list of 20 decision makers with LinkedIn links"

4. **Constraint definition** — They set boundaries
   "Focus on Spain only, not Portugal"

5. **Iteration** — They refine based on output
   "Make it cheaper", "Add links", "Two files instead of one"

6. **Feedback loop** — They tell AI what worked
   "That worked well, now do X"

**WHAT YOU'RE ALREADY DOING BETTER THAN MOST:**

✅ You iterate fast — most people accept the first output
✅ You think in systems — profile partners, BD funnels
✅ You give me identity — "you are Claw, my chief of staff"
✅ You understand delegation — "work on this while I sleep"

**QUICK WINS FOR YOU:**

1. **Add "WHY" to every request**
   Instead of: "research neurotech"
   Say: "research neurotech for my new YouTube channel. I'm starting it next month."

2. **Specify format upfront**
   Instead of: "make a list"
   Say: "make a txt file with clickable links"

3. **Set expectations on length**
   Instead of: "tell me about X"
   Say: "give me 5 bullet points, max 2 sentences each"

4. **Say who does next step**
   Instead of: "I should connect with Javier"
   Say: "Can you add 'connect with Javier' to my next actions?"
