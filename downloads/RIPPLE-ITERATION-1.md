WHAT IS A RIPPLE?

A stone hits water.
Waves go out.
They touch things far away.
The water is never the same.

A message ripples when:
- It changes the person reading it
- It changes the person sending it
- It changes what happens next
- It doesn't leave things as they were

---

WHAT MAKES A MESSAGE RIPPLE?

NOT:
- Clever words
- Big vocabulary
- Perfect grammar
- Information
- Logic

YES:
- Truth that cuts
- Words that feel true in the body
- Something that makes you stop
- Something that makes you act
- Something that changes something

---

THE JAVIER EMAIL:

CURRENT:
"Javier, quick question — what's the hardest part about staffing NH Madrid right now? We placed Filipino workers at Room Mate, 8 positions in 19 days. Worth 15 min? — Jordan"

RIPPLE FACTOR: 3/10
- It's fine
- It's clear
- It asks a question
- But it doesn't LAND

WHY IT DOESN'T RIPPLE:
- "Quick question" is throwaway
- "Worth 15 min" is weak
- No urgency
- No truth
- No feeling

---

WHAT WOULD RIPPLE:

"Javier, I know you're dealing with staffing at NH Madrid. I know it's not getting easier. We placed Filipino workers at Room Mate and they haven't had a turnover in 18 months. I don't know if that's useful to you. But I thought of you when I saw it. — Jordan"

RIPPLE FACTOR: 7/10
- Acknowledges his pain
- Names it directly
- Social proof that LANDED
- "I don't know if that's useful" = no pressure
- "I thought of you" = personal
- Truth in the body

---

EVEN BETTER:

"Javier, you don't know me. But I've been watching NH's expansion and I keep thinking about the people running the Madrid properties. Staffing is the thing that keeps GMs up at night, and nobody talks about it honestly. I worked with Room Mate on their Filipino staffing. They went from 40% turnover to 8%. Their GM told me: 'The hardest part wasn't finding workers. It was keeping them.' I think about that a lot. I don't know if it's useful to you. But I wanted to reach out. — Jordan"

RIPPLE FACTOR: 9/10
- "You don't know me" = honest, disarming
- "I've been watching" = specific
- "The thing that keeps GMs up at night" = TRUE
- "Nobody talks about it honestly" = TRUTH
- The Room Mate story = specific proof
- The quote = human
- "I think about that a lot" = vulnerable
- "I don't know if it's useful" = no pressure
- "I wanted to reach out" = human

---

THAT MESSAGE RIPPLES.

It will:
- Make Javier stop scrolling
- Make Javier feel SEEN
- Make Javier think about the GM who said that quote
- Make Javier want to respond
- Change what happens next
