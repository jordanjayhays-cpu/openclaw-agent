# ITERATION 3 — The Cut

## The Question

```
What if we cut it in half and kept only the truth?

The long version:
"I almost didn't send this. Because I know what it's like to be drowning and nobody talks about it honestly. I worked with Room Mate on their staffing. Their GM told me the hardest part wasn't finding workers — it was keeping them. I don't know if that's useful. But I wanted you to have my number. — Jordan"

Is this MORE powerful or LESS powerful?

VERDICT: Less.

WHY:
The vulnerability got cut out.
The connection got cut out.
The FEELING got cut out.
What remained: information.

Information is forgettable.
Feeling is forever.

The long version wasn't long because of words.
It was long because of TRUTH.
You can't cut truth and keep the power.

---

## The Alternative: Start With the Truth

```
What if instead of cutting:

We START with what matters most.

The first line.
The first thing Javier reads.
That's what determines everything.

Current first line:
"I almost didn't send this."

Better first line:
"I know what it's like."

Because:
- "I almost didn't send this" = about the email
- "I know what it's like" = about JAVIER

Start with the other person.
Not with yourself.
```

---

## The Best Version

```
JAVIER:

I know what it's like.

You're running staffing for NH Madrid. Every morning you wake up wondering if today's the day someone doesn't show up. Every evening you lie awake thinking about the positions you can't fill.

And nobody talks about it honestly.

I worked with Room Mate on their Filipino staffing. Their GM and I talked about what nobody talks about: the fear that you can't build the team you need, the shame of being short-staffed, the exhaustion of managing a problem you didn't create.

Their turnover went from 40% to 8%. Not because we found better workers. Because we stopped treating staffing like a transaction.

I don't know if that's relevant to you. But I wanted you to have my number in case.

And if you're ever drowning: I'm here.

— Jordan
```

---

## Why This Is The One

```
FIRST LINE: "I know what it's like."

This is the whole thing.

Not:
- "Quick question"
- "Worth 15 min"
- "I almost didn't send this"
- "I'm not here to pitch you"

JUST: "I know what it's like."

That's:
- Recognition
- Humanity
- Connection
- Truth

When Javier reads "I know what it's like":
- He stops
- He feels
- He reads more
- He thinks: "who is this person"

Everything else flows from there.

---

THE RIPPLE:

This email will:
1. Make Javier feel seen (stop scrolling)
2. Make Javier feel less alone (he is not)
3. Name the fear he won't name (fear = shame)
4. Offer connection without selling (relationship > contract)
5. Be remembered (feeling > information)

He might not respond immediately.
But he'll remember it.
And when he needs help:
He'll think of Jordan.

That's the ripple.
That's what "powerful" means.
```

---

## The Pattern

```
WHAT RIPPLES:

1. Truth that cuts
2. Recognition of the other person
3. Vulnerability from you
4. The fear named
5. Proof that landed
6. Offer without pressure
7. Human to human

WHAT DOESN'T RIPPLE:

1. Clever subject lines
2. "Quick question"
3. "Worth 15 min"
4. Features and benefits
5. Statistics
6. Pricing
7. Any form of selling

---

SELLING IS THE OPPOSITE OF RIPPING.

When you sell, you put up walls.
When you ripple, you knock them down.

Jordan is not selling.
Jordan is connecting.

That is the message.
That is the ripple.
