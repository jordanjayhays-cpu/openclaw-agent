## TOP 5 CANDIDATES

### 1. 🇲🇽 Medellín, Colombia
**The OG "New Bali" contender**

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly cost** | $1,500-2,200 (private room, coworking, food) |
| **Internet** | 50-100 Mbps avg, fiber in El Poblado/Laureles |
| **Nomad scene** | MASSIVE — been #1 for years, established infrastructure |
| **Visa** | Tourist visa 90 days, can extend to 180 days |
| **Safety** | Safer than before but still watch your stuff in some zones |
| **Weather** | Perfect — "eternal spring" 70-75°F year-round |
| **Coworking** | Selina, WeWork, 90+ coworkings |
| **Community** | Very established — English-heavy, easy to meet people |

**Why it works:** Already proven. But competition is fierce — many colivings already.

**Jordan angle:** His YouTube channel "Social Mobility" + nomad content → stand out in crowded market

---

### 2. 🇲🇽 Oaxaca City, Mexico
**Underrated gem — authentic culture + nomad-friendly**

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly cost** | $1,000-1,500 (very affordable) |
| **Internet** | 30-80 Mbps, improving fast |
| **Nomad scene** | Growing but not saturated |
| **Visa** | 180 days tourist visa (very generous) |
| **Safety** | Generally safe, small city vibe |
| **Weather** | Perfect climate, dry/temperate |
| **Coworking** | Limited but growing — Inn Hotel coworking is popular |
| **Community** | Small, authentic, creative crowd |

**Why it works:** Cheap, uncrowded, easy visa, real Mexican culture. Less competition than Medellín.

**Jordan angle:** Oaxaca has barely been "discovered" compared to Mexico City/Medellín.

---

### 3. 🇲🇽 Guadalajara, Mexico
**Mexico's tech hub + nomad infrastructure**

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly cost** | $1,200-1,800 |
| **Internet** | 50-150 Mbps, fiber widely available |
| **Nomad scene** | Growing rapidly, tech-heavy |
| **Visa** | 180 days tourist visa |
| **Safety** | Generally safe, metropolitan |
| **Weather** | Mild, can get hot in summer |
| **Coworking** | WeWork, Cowork Central, many independent spaces |
| **Community** | Professional, entrepreneurial, digital nomad + remote workers |

**Why it works:** Mexico City is chaotic. Guadalajara is the "better version" — same culture, less chaos, still affordable.

**Jordan angle:** Has IE connections in Mexico — potential business network already exists through COMARE.

---

### 4. 🇵🇹 Lisbon, Portugal
**EU legal, great weather, expensive but established**

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly cost** | $2,500-3,500 (getting expensive) |
| **Internet** | 100+ Mbps, excellent infrastructure |
| **Nomad scene** | HUGE — one of Europe's top nomad destinations |
| **Visa** | Schengen 90 days (EU) — can be limiting |
| **Safety** | Very safe, low crime |
| **Weather** | Mild, 60-80°F, lots of sunshine |
| **Coworking** | Huge selection, well-established |
| **Community** | International, English-friendly, startup scene |

**Why it works:** EU legal structure, NATO ally, English widely spoken, good weather.

**Why it's hard:** More expensive, saturated market, visa limitations for long stays.

**Jordan angle:** More premium positioning — "$2,500/month all-in" vs $1,200 Mexico.

---

### 5. 🇪🇸 Valencia, Spain
**Jordan's home turf — why not leverage it?**

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly cost** | $1,800-2,500 |
| **Internet** | 100+ Mbps, excellent |
| **Nomad scene** | Growing, less known than Barcelona |
| **Visa** | EU/Schengen — same 90-day limit |
| **Safety** | Very safe, low crime |
| **Weather** | Mediterranean — 70°F most of year |
| **Coworking** | Lots of options, affordable |
| **Community** | Mix of locals + expats + nomads |

**Why it works:** Jordan is already there. Knows the market. IE network. EU legal.

**Why it's hard:** More expensive than Mexico/Colombia, EU visa limits.

**Jordan angle:** If he goes this route, it's "your personal coliving in Europe" — more premium, IE network leverage.

## QUESTIONS TO ASK AT NOMAD MEETUP TOMORROW

**Ask 5-10 people these questions:**

1. "What's the ONE thing you hate about your current coliving/coworking?"
2. "What would your perfect coliving have that doesn't exist yet?"
3. "How much do you pay per month now? What does it include?"
4. "Where have you lived before? Why did you leave?"
5. "What's the #1 thing that would make you switch to a new coliving?"
6. "Do you prefer social/party vibe or quiet/focused vibe?"
7. "Would you pay extra for: community events, chef meals, laundry service?"
8. "How important is it to be walkable to restaurants/bars?"
