# THE PEN — Iterations

## The Principle

```
"Sell me this pen"

Not: Explain why the pen is good.
Not: Tell me features.
Not: Pitch me.

Ask: What do you need?
Create: The need.
Close: The gap.

---

The pen exercise works because:
- It finds the need first
- It creates urgency
- It makes THEM want it

The best answer:
"Can you sign your name on this napkin?" *hands pen*
"I just sold you that pen"

The sale happened when HE needed it.
```

---

## Iteration 1 — The Question

```
Javier, what's your staffing challenge?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Ask first. Listen. Then help.

---

## Iteration 2 — The Observation + Question

```
Javier, I see NH Madrid is expanding. What's the hardest part?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Signal that I see him. Ask about the challenge.

---

## Iteration 3 — The Vulnerability Hook

```
Javier, I'm building something similar. What's your staffing problem?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** I have skin in the game. We're the same. Ask what he needs.

---

## Iteration 4 — The Specific + Open

```
Javier, staffing for NH Madrid — what's not working?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Specific hotel. Open wound. Short.

---

## Iteration 5 — The Direct Ask

```
Javier, what do you need?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** No context. No fluff. Just: what do you need.

---

## Iteration 6 — The Pattern Interrupt

```
Javier, skip the small talk. What staffing problem keeps you up at night?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Different tone. Direct. No pretense.

---

## Iteration 7 — The One-Liner

```
Javier - what's broken?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Shortest possible. Brutal honesty.

---

## Iteration 8 — The "I Know You" (if warm)

```
Javier, you're dealing with the same thing I help hotels solve. What's your version of it?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Shows I understand. Asks for his version.

---

## Iteration 9 — The Offer

```
Javier, I help hotels solve staffing. Want to tell me yours?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Name what I do. Ask him to pitch.

---

## Iteration 10 — The Close (For After He Responds)

```
So the problem is X. And if I could solve X — would you want to know how?

— Jordan
```

**Principle:** Get commitment. Make him say yes to learning more.

---

## What To Send

```
JAVIER:

Javier, what staffing challenge keeps you up at night?

— Jordan
```

---

## Why This Works

```
"Quick question worth 15 min" = vendor
"I know what it's like" = too much, too soon
"What's your staffing challenge?" = curious

He's not a prospect.
He's a person.
With a problem.
That I might help with.

Ask. Listen. Then offer.

The ripple starts with listening.
```
