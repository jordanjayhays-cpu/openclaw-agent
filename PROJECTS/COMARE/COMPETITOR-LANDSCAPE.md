## WHY THIS MATTERS

You need to know who's already in the door BEFORE you pitch. If a prospect has a satisfied maintenance vendor, your pitch is a harder sell. If they're suffering with fragmented vendors or the local plumber, COMARE is a category creation, not a competitor take.

**Key insight:** Most small gyms and independent pharmacies in Mexico City are NOT being served by professional B2B maintenance companies. They're using:
- The local plumber they call when something leaks
- The neighborhood electrician
- The building's in-house handyman
- Emergency calls to whoever picks up

**COMARE's real competitor is fragmentation and DIY vendor management, not other companies.**

## TIER 1: Industrial/Commercial Giants (NOT Direct Competitors)

These companies serve large industrial facilities, factories, and big-box commercial — not small gyms and pharmacy chains. They're too expensive and too slow for the ICP.

### Grupo CICE
- **What they do:** Industrial maintenance, large-scale facility management
- **Clients:** Manufacturing plants, large warehouses, industrial parks
- **Why NOT a threat:** Too big/industrial for gym and pharmacy ICP. Minimum contract sizes way above what small gyms pay.
- **Price range:** Enterprise-level contracts

### IMSUMAQ (Instrumentación y Mantenimiento)
- **What they do:** Industrial instrumentation, specialized equipment maintenance
- **Clients:** Oil & gas, heavy manufacturing
- **Why NOT a threat:** Specialized industrial focus. Not retail/commercial maintenance.

### Grupo Proa
- **What they do:** Large-scale property/facility management
- **Clients:** Corporate towers, shopping centers, large office buildings
- **Why NOT a threat:** Corporate real estate focus. Minimum contract sizes high. They want 10,000+ sq ft buildings.

## TIER 3: Informal / Local Vendors (THE REAL COMPETITOR)

This is who COMARE actually competes against. Fragmented, reactive, local.

### The Local Plumber
- **Who:** Individual plumber in each neighborhood
- **How they win:** Someone at the gym knows a guy. Personal relationship.
- **Their advantage:** Fast local response, personal relationship, cheap emergency callouts
- **Their weakness:** No HVAC, no electrical, no preventive maintenance. Only fixes what's broken.
- **Pain for prospect:** Emergency calls at 2am. Different person for every trade.
- **COMARE counter:** One number. Every trade. Preventive so emergencies stop.

### The Building Handyman
- **Who:** Building maintenance person employed by the property owner
- **How they win:** Already paid for. Building owner handles it.
- **Their weakness:** Only handles basic stuff. Not specialized trades. Not available 24/7.
- **Pain for prospect:** Building handyman can't fix industrial kitchen equipment or HVAC.
- **COMARE counter:** 400+ service points. Licensed specialists in every trade.

### Emergency Service Call Centers
- **Who:** Companies like GRUPO REFRIEG or emergency plumbing services
- **How they win:** Prospect Googles "plomero 24 horas CDMX" when something breaks
- **Their weakness:** Expensive (emergency premium = 2-3x), reactive only, no relationship
- **Pain for prospect:** $500+ per emergency call. No accountability for prevention.
- **COMARE counter:** Fixed monthly. Preventive visits prevent the emergency call entirely.

### Independent Electricians
- **Who:** Individual electricians working the gym/restaurant circuit
- **How they win:** Word of mouth in the neighborhood business community
- **Their weakness:** No licensing verification, no insurance, no HVAC, no plumbing
- **Pain for prospect:** Equipment failures, no recourse when the work is bad
- **COMARE counter:** Licensed, insured, accountable. One vendor for every trade.

## TIER 4: The "We Have a Guy" Competitor

This is the most insidious competitor — the prospect's own status quo. It's not a company, it's a habit.

### "We manage it ourselves"
- **What this means:** The gym owner or pharmacy regional manager personally coordinates 5+ vendors
- **Why they do it:** "It's easier to just call my guy"
- **The hidden cost:** 20 hours/month of their time at $50-100/hr = $1,000-2,000/month in management cost
- **COMARE counter:** "You spend $1,000/month in your own time managing vendors. COMARE costs less than that and you never make another call."

### Franchise-provided maintenance (for chains)
- **What this means:** Some franchises have preferred vendor lists managed at HQ
- **Why they do it:** Corporate compliance, brand standards
- **Their weakness:** Franchisees often ignore the preferred vendor list because the prices are too high
- **COMARE counter:** Flexibility and pricing that works for the franchisee, not just the franchisor.
