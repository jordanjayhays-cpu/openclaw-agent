# EGO DEATH — 5-Minute Provocative Speech

**Time:** 5 minutes
**Word count:** ~700 words
**Tone:** Provocative, vulnerable, bold

---

## OPENING HOOK (0:00-1:00)

*[Stand center. Silence. Make eye contact. Then speak quietly.]*

"I once asked a billionaire what he wanted next.
He said: 'One trillion dollars.'
I asked: 'And then?'
He stared at me. He had no answer."

*[PAUSE 3 seconds]*

"That man is the most successful failure I ever met."

*[Make eye contact with someone in the audience.]*

"Your grades, your bank account, your title — these are the high horse.
And the high horse will kill you."

---

## THE PROBLEM (1:00-2:30)

"Ego is a bodyguard.
It guards us from the one truth we can't handle:
We are going to die.

The ego's job is simple: lie to us.
Tell us we're special. Tell us we'll live forever.
Tell us the money matters."

*[Walk left.]*

"I used to be on that high horse.
Every presentation, every conversation — I was calculating:
What do they think of me?
Am I winning?"

*[PAUSE 2 seconds]*

"The high horse feels safe.
Until it throws you."

---

## THE EXPERIENCE (2:30-4:00)

*[Voice drops. Slower.]*

"Three years ago, I watched a friend nearly die.
Medical emergency. Twenty minutes of uncertainty.
In that twenty minutes, I felt my ego collapse completely.

No titles. No reputation. No future plans that mattered.
Just: 'Is he alive?'

And here's what I found on the other side of that collapse:

 stillness.
 A quiet I've never found in meditation.
 A connection to something larger than my own survival."

*[PAUSE 2 seconds]*

"Ego death isn't pretty.
It's painful. It's terrifying.
Mike Tyson described it as 'the death of a thousand cuts.'
But those cuts? They're the only thing that lets the real you breathe."

---

## THE JOURNEY (4:00-4:45)

"You want ego death?
Try this:

Put your entire identity in one external goal.
Then watch it fail.
Watch the cruise ship break down.
Watch the 'time out' become 30 days of silence.
Hear the voices of the future — your own fears — and sit with them.

Or: don't.
Stay on the high horse.
But the horse is tired.
And it's heading somewhere you don't want to go."

---

## CLOSING (4:45-5:00)

*[Stand still. Quiet voice.]*

"There's a consciousness pod.
Inside it: everything you are without the armor.
Without the grades, the money, the opinions of others.

Most people never enter it.
They're too busy protecting it."

*[Make direct eye contact. Pause 4 seconds.]*

"You could.
Right now.
If you get off the horse."

*[Silence. Exit.]*

---

## DELIVERY NOTES

| When | Do |
|------|---|
| Opening | Start quiet. Let silence work. |
| "One trillion dollars" | Say it like a fact, not a question |
| First pause | Hold it. Eye contact. Make them wait. |
| "Most successful failure" | Slight smile. Let it land. |
| Ego as bodyguard | Slight edge in voice. They're guilty too. |
| Walk left | On the "high horse" line. Own the space. |
| Near death story | Slow down. Vulnerable. Real. |
| "Stillness" | Whisper. Intimate. |
| Closing | Back to center. Stillness. Let it breathe. |

## WHAT TO AVOID

- Don't rush the opening
- Don't laugh at your own jokes
- Don't gesture too much — let stillness be the movement
- Don't raise voice at the end — the quiet is the power
- Don't smile during the problem section — this is serious
- Don't look at your notes
