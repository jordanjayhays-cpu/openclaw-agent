## Concept
Japanese philosophy — beauty in imperfection, impermanence, the cracked bowl is more valuable than the pristine one. The crack is where the light enters. Beauty in things half-broken. Completion is a fiction.

## Theme
Imperfection as freedom. The tyranny of "perfect." Letting go of the flawless idea. Transience as gift, not threat. Beauty lives in the worn, the aged, the incomplete. Embracing the crack.

## Key References
- Wabi-sabi: beauty in imperfection and impermanence
- "The crack is where the light enters" — Rumi / the cracked vessel
- Kintsugi — golden repair, the break as part of the story
- Mono no aware — pathos of things, the tear at impermanence
- The aged maple, the moss-covered stone, the chipped tea bowl
