## 1. Top 5 Competitors & Positioning

### 1.1 John Clements Recruitment (JCR)
- **HQ:** Manila, with offices across Asia-Pacific and Europe
- **Positioning:** Premium executive/managerial search for hospitality, tourism, F&B
- **USP:** ISO-certified, global candidate database, dedicated hospitality division
- **Presence in Spain:** Active placement partner with major hotel groups (NH, Meliá referenced in their case studies)
- **Typical Roles:** GM, F&B Director, Executive Chef, Revenue Manager
- **Pricing:** 20-25% of annual base salary (contingency); 30-35% (retained)
- **Weakness:** High-volume/hourly hospitality roles not their focus

### 1.2 CGlobal (Candidate Global)
- **HQ:** Madrid-based, Filipino-founded
- **Positioning:** Spain-focused Filipino talent acquisition — hospitality, domestic, care sectors
- **USP:** Bilingual (Spanish-Filipino cultural bridge), on-the-ground in Spain, visa sponsorship knowledge
- **Typical Roles:** Housekeeping staff, front desk, kitchen support, hospitality assistants
- **Pricing:** Estimated 18-22% markup on candidate salary or flat placement fee (€800-1,500 per placement)
- **Weakness:** Smaller database, slower volume placements, limited EU-wide reach

### 1.3 ManpowerGroup Philippines
- **HQ:** Global (Milwaukee) — Philippines franchise
- **Positioning:** Volume staffing, batch placements for hotels/cruise lines/corporate
- **USP:** Massive database, bulk processing, compliance/contractor management
- **Typical Roles:** Mass hires for hotel openings, banquets, seasonal staff
- **Pricing:** 15-20% on annual salary; volume discounts for 20+ placements
- **Weakness:** Commoditized, slow for niche/hospitality-specialist roles, less personalized

### 1.4 IQI Global (formerly JAC Recruitment Japan)
- **HQ:** Tokyo/Manila, European desk in Spain
- **Positioning:** Professional/managerial placements with Japan-linked hospitality groups
- **USP:** Strong in Japanese-owned hotels in Europe (Nikko, Ritz-Carlton Japan ties, Mitsui), multilingual professionals
- **Typical Roles:** Bilingual managers, Japanese-speaking front office, Asian cuisine chefs
- **Pricing:** 22-28% of annual salary
- **Weakness:** Niche to Japanese-speaking roles; high price point

### 1.5 JobsGlobal.com / Phil-Aspac Recruitment
- **HQ:** Manila, EU desk
- **Positioning:** Filipino workers for European hospitality — Spain, Italy, Germany
- **USP:** POEA-licensed, experience with European work visa processes, cost-effective
- **Typical Roles:** Hotel staff, restaurant crew, kitchen staff, cleaners
- **Pricing:** Reported 15-20% markup or placement fees ranging €500-1,200
- **Weakness:** More volume/blue-collar focused, less premium positioning, less hospitality-specific

## 2. Pricing Ranges (Industry Standard)

| Model | Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Contingency search | 15-25% of annual salary | Most common for professional roles |
| Retained search | 25-35% of annual salary | For GM/Director level, confidential searches |
| Flat placement fee | €500-2,000 | Common for hourly/volume roles |
| Contract staffing margin | 20-40% on hourly rate | For temp/trial period placements |
| Trial period guarantee | 30-90 days | Most agencies offer replacement if candidate leaves |

**PISCO-specific opportunity:** Most agencies price on annual salary. For hotels, entry-level staff (€18-25K salary) with 20% markup = €3,600-5,000 per placement. Mid-manager (€35-50K) = €7,000-12,500. PISCO can compete on **speed + niche hospitality focus + volume discounts** rather than price alone.

## 3. Gaps They Don't Fill

### 3.1 Spanish-Language Integration Support
None of the agencies above provide structured Spanish language training or cultural onboarding for Filipino hires. This is a major gap — hotels want candidates who can communicate in basic Spanish from day 1.

**PISCO opportunity:** Offer pre-arrival Spanish language bootcamp + cultural integration package as a value-add.

### 3.2 Batched/Seasonal Staffing for Resorts
Hotels in Spain (Costa del Sol, Canary Islands, Balearics) have extreme seasonal peaks (April-September). No agency offers **seasonal staffing packages** — dedicated pre-screened Filipino talent ready for 3-6 month contracts.

**PISCO opportunity:** Create a "summer ready" talent pool with guaranteed availability windows.

### 3.3 Long-term Retention Programs
All agencies treat placement as the end of the transaction. None offer **90/180/365-day retention check-ins** to reduce turnover — which is hotels' #1 pain point with Filipino staff.

**PISCO opportunity:** Retention-as-a-service — follow-up calls, mediation, career pathing for placed candidates.

### 3.4 Filipino-Specific Visa/NRIC Pathway Guidance
Spain's work visa process for non-EU nationals is complex. No agency provides step-by-step case management through:
- TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) process
- EPS (Empadronamiento) registration
- Social security enrollment for Filipino workers

**PISCO opportunity:** White-glove visa/relocation support as differentiator.

### 3.5 Small/Hboutique Hotel Coverage
All major agencies focus on chains (NH, Iberostar, Meliá, Marriott). Independent boutique hotels (which make up 60%+ of Spanish hotels) are underserved — they can't afford retained search fees but need quality staff.

**PISCO opportunity:** Affordable retainer-light model for boutique hotels (3-5 staff minimum).

### 3.6 Multi-Country EU Placement
Most agencies are Spain-only or EU-only. No one offers a **pan-European placement pathway** for Filipino staff — e.g., trial period in Spain → transfer to Germany/France with proper documentation.

**PISCO opportunity:** Build a Europe-wide network so one Filipino hire can serve multiple hotel partners across borders.

## 4. Partnership Opportunities

### 4.1 Recruitment Aggregators
- **LinkedIn (Recruiter Lite):** Target "hospitality recruitment Spain" agencies and connect
- **Bizneo HR:** Spanish HR software used by 1,000+ hotels — potential co-marketing or referral partnership
- **Personio:** EU SME HR platform, many hotel clients; affiliate/referral relationship

### 4.2 Hospitality Associations
- **CEOH (Confederación Española de Hoteles y Alojamientos Turísticos):** Industry body for Spanish hotels
- **FEHP (Federación Española de Hostelería):** Restaurant/hotel federation — speaks for 300,000+ establishments
- **HOTREC (Europe):** EU hospitality association — pan-European reach

### 4.3 Filipino Diaspora Organizations
- **Filipino Community in Spain:** Can serve as informal recruitment channels and referral source
- **Kabataan:** Youth Filipino diaspora network
- **OFW (Overseas Filipino Workers) organizations:** Already have trust networks; partnership could yield warm referrals

### 4.4 Training & Visa Partners
- **Instituto Cervantes:** Spanish language certification — potential co-branding for language prep
- **Meridian Education (Manila):** Hospitality training provider — could be PISCO's Manila sourcing arm
- **EU immigration lawyers:** Partner with specialists in Madrid/Barcelona for visa processing (use as sub-contractors)

### 4.5 Hotel Tech Platforms
- **Cloudbeds, Mews, RoomRaccoon:** Hotel PMS systems — many have HR modules or can recommend PISCO as staffing partner
- **Zoku, Limecraft:** Co-living/corporate housing platforms — could be referral for staff housing needs

## 5. Quick Competitive Positioning for PISCO

Based on above, PISCO's unique angle should be:

> **"The only Filipino staffing partner that treats placement as the start — not the end — of the relationship."**

PISCO differentiates on:
1. **Speed** — pre-vetted Filipino hospitality talent pool ready in 2-3 weeks (vs. 6-8 weeks industry average)
2. **Retention focus** — 365-day retention guarantee with check-ins and mediation
3. **Niche hospitality** — not generalist staffing; Filipino staff only, hotels/hospitality only
4. **Integration support** — Spanish language prep + cultural onboarding included
5. **Volume pricing** — flat fees for 5+ placement packs (boutique hotels' sweet spot)
