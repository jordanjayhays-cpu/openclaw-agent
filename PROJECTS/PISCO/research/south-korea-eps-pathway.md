## TL;DR

South Korea is a **viable expansion market** for PISCO, but the pathway is different from Spain:

- **EPS (Employment Permit System)** = E-9 visa = manual/labor/s hospitality workers, NOT hotel professionals
- **E-7 visa** = skilled/professional workers = hotel staff possible, but requires employer sponsorship + Korean language
- **Key insight:** For hotel/hospitality roles (front desk, housekeeping, F&B), E-7 is the right visa. EPS is for factory/agriculture workers.

**PISCO opportunity:** Filipino staff for Korean hotels (Lotte, Shilla, Marriott Korea, Hyatt Korea, Hilton Korea) via E-7 pathway. No direct government-to-government channel needed for E-7 — it's employer-driven.

## South Korea Visa Pathways for Hospitality Workers

### E-7 Visa — Special Ability (Professional Worker)
**Best for:** Hotel receptionists, managers, chefs, F&B staff

**Requirements:**
- Job offer from a Korean company (employer-sponsored)
- The job must match one of 87 E-7 categories
- For hotels: "Hotel Receptionist" and "Accommodation-Related Manager" are specific E-7 categories
- Korean language ability strengthens the application (no mandatory requirement but helps)
- Degree or relevant experience in the field
- Employer must file with Ministry of Justice

**Process:**
1. Employer gets a "Letter of Recommendation for Employment" from relevant Korean gov body
2. Employee applies for E-7 at Korean embassy in Manila
3. Processing time: ~2-4 weeks

**Key hotels that hire foreigners:**
- Lotte Hotels & Resorts (Korean brand, Seoul/Busan presence)
- Shilla Hotels & Resorts (luxury, Samsung group)
- Marriott Korea (Seoul, Busan)
- Hyatt Regency Seoul
- The Westin Josun Seoul

**Salary range for hotel staff:**
- Entry-level (front desk/housekeeping): ₩20-30M/year (~$15-22K USD)
- Mid-manager: ₩35-55M/year (~$26-41K USD)
- E-7 minimum salary threshold: ₩36M/year (2025 standard, subject to change)

### EPS (Employment Permit System) — E-9 Visa
**NOT suitable for hotel work** — this is for manufacturing, construction, agriculture, fisheries. Filipino workers using EPS go to factories. Korean hotels do not use EPS to hire foreigners.

However, EPS could be a **backdoor consideration** for kitchen staff who might qualify under hospitality-adjacent roles. Low priority.

## Key Korean Hospitality Market Facts

- **Labor shortage:** Statistics Korea confirms in-person service industry (lodging/restaurant) has significant labor shortage — Korean workers shifted to transportation/warehousing
- **International hotels in Korea** actively recruit foreign staff, especially for international-facing roles (concierge, international front desk)
- **Korean language requirement** is a real barrier — most Filipino candidates would need K-language training
- **Seasonal tourism:** Korea has peaks (cherry blossom spring, summer beach resorts, autumn foliage, winter ski resorts) — similar seasonal model to Spanish hotels

NOT factory/hospitality workers via EPS — those go to factories.

### Target Hotels (Outreach Priority)

2. **Shilla Hotels & Resorts** — luxury, Samsung ecosystem
3. **Marriott Korea** — international standards, already familiar with Filipino staff
4. **Hyatt Regency Seoul** — international brand, global standards
5. **Grand Hyatt Seoul** — large property, high staff turnover

### Recruitment Channel
- E-7 requires **employer sponsorship** — so outreach goes to HR at these hotel groups
- No government-to-government agreement needed (unlike EPS for factory workers)
- PISCO's role: source pre-vetted Filipino candidates, help with documentation, provide Korean language prep resources

### Differentiation from Spain Market
- Spain: NH, Iberostar, Meliá — large Spanish chains with known hiring patterns
- Korea: Lotte, Shilla, Marriott Korea — Korean/international chains with different HR structures
- Common thread: both need Filipino hospitality staff for seasonal and permanent roles
