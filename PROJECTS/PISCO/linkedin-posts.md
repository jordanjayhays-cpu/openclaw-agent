## Post 3: Hotel HR Tip — Retention

**One retention strategy most hotels ignore completely: career architecture**

Here's what I see repeatedly:

Hotel hires someone great → They excel → They hit a ceiling → They leave.

The hotel wonders why they can't keep "good people."

The answer: you didn't give them a reason to stay.

Retention isn't about ping pong tables and free lunches. It's about:

1. **Visible growth paths** — What does year 2 look like? Year 5?
2. **Skill certification** — Can they learn something that travels with them?
3. **Genuine recognition** — Not just "good job" but "we see what you built here"
4. **Cross-property mobility** — Can they transfer to another location if they need to relocate?

Filipino hospitality workers — specifically — are looking for exactly this. They're building careers, not just doing jobs.

Hotels that offer real career architecture don't just retain Filipino staff. They retain the BEST Filipino staff.

And word travels. Good retention creates talent pipelines. Bad retention burns them.

What's your retention rate in year 1? Year 3? I'd genuinely like to know.
